# Advanced: Problem Solving Under Uncertainty

When time periods are long, complexity is high, and consequences of error are substantial, the seven-step process still applies but requires additional frameworks.

## Levels of Uncertainty

| Level | Description | Example | Time to Resolve |
|-------|-------------|---------|-----------------|
| **1** | Reasonably predictable | Short-term forecasts, stable markets | Near-term |
| **2** | Alternative futures (binary) | Regulatory change, technology adoption | 5-10 years |
| **3** | Range of futures | Energy mix in 15 years, AI impact on jobs | 10-20 years |
| **4** | True ambiguity | Sea levels in 2050, early-stage biotech | Decades+ |
| **5** | Unknown unknowns | Truly unpredictable events | Indeterminate |

Levels 1-4 are "known unknowns." Level 5 is the "unknown unknown."

**Key insight**: Uncertainty can be a good thing for strategic problem solvers. If your analysis is better than others', you can earn good returns while others flounder.

## Actions Toolkit for Uncertainty

Match your actions to the uncertainty level:

### Buy Information
Gather data and analysis to clarify uncertainty sources. At higher levels, this means modeling distant events rather than just gathering observable facts. There's a point where more information costs more than it's worth.

### Hedges
Make reasonable-cost investments to counter downside events. Example: Fossil fuel companies investing in renewables. Financial hedges (options, futures) work for shorter-term problems.

### Low-Cost Strategic Options
Bet on multiple horses. Make small investments across competing approaches to learn and build optionality. IBM's failure to exercise its options in Intel and Microsoft is a cautionary tale — a $300M Microsoft investment would have been worth $33B a decade later.

### Insurance
Pay a premium to cover catastrophic downside. Climate change is sometimes framed as an insurance problem — ~1% of global GDP to keep warming below 2°C.

### No-Regrets Moves
Actions that make sense regardless of how uncertainty resolves. Usually involves building capabilities you'll need no matter what happens.

### Big Bets
Only when you have confidence about an outcome not shared by others, AND you have a business model that generates cash with low debt. Big bets can be shaping strategies — but they can also create stranded assets when uncertainty resolves unfavorably.

## Strategic Staircases (Growth Horizons)

For building new businesses or entering uncertain markets, use a step-by-step staircase approach:

### Architecture
1. **Work backward from the goal.** What capabilities, assets, and learning are needed?
2. **Take measured steps.** Expand commitment as uncertainty reduces.
3. **Frame in time.** Map staircase steps, knowledge captured, and capabilities built.

### Three Considerations
- **Stretch**: How far is each step from current capabilities? Balance absorption capacity vs. competitive speed.
- **Momentum**: Early success builds learning and confidence. Critical in winner-take-all markets.
- **Flexibility**: Maintain optionality. Pick step-outs that generate the most option value for follow-on initiatives. Avoid creating sunk assets that could become stranded.

### Planning Approach
- Agree in detail on steps for the next 12-18 months
- Take stock, then plan the next 12-18 months as new information arrives
- Adjust the end goal or timing to reflect market and competitive realities
- Embrace agile development, failing fast, and sprints

## Long-Term Investment Valuation

For long-lived assets (mines, infrastructure, major acquisitions):

1. **Estimate NPV of cash flows** from the existing asset
2. **Add a branch for development options** — future projects enabled by this investment
3. **Identify key uncertainties** (price, volume, exchange rates, regulation)
4. **Build a scenario matrix** — best case, likely case, worst case, disaster case
5. **Calculate joint probabilities** — if each bad outcome has 10% chance, both occurring together is only 1%
6. **Do look-backs** — review past investment decisions to calibrate your process

## Wicked Problems

Some problems seem to defy definition because they involve:
- Multiple interconnected causes
- Major values disagreements among stakeholders
- Unintended consequences of interventions
- Substantial behavior change requirements

### Approach for Wicked Problems
1. **The seven steps still apply** — they just require more patience and iteration
2. **Use a Theory of Change (TOC) map** — visualize the overall strategic logic
3. **Build a portfolio of strategies** — balance transformational bets with incremental moves
4. **Manage across stages**: Seed → Cultivate → Harvest
5. **Accept that some strategies will compete with each other** — that's by design
6. **Set a vision that nearly everyone can agree on** — helps warring parties find common ground
7. **Map supporting and competing strategies** to manage the portfolio

## Tips for Helping Users with Uncertainty

- First, help them assess the uncertainty level (1-5) for each key variable
- Match actions to uncertainty levels — don't make big bets at Level 4
- Ask: "What actions make sense regardless of how this plays out?" (no-regrets moves)
- Help them identify low-cost options to maintain flexibility
- For career/life decisions: Use the strength/interest/risk-tolerance matrix
- For investment decisions: Build a payoff table with multiple scenarios and joint probabilities
- Remind them: Defer irreversible commitments when uncertainty is high (unless preemption risk is real)
